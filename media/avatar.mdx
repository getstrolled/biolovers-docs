## Upload

- Tap the file picker and choose an image.
- Formats: **JPEG / PNG / WebP / GIF**.
- Max **2 MiB** (≈ 2 MB).
- The image is auto-cropped to a circle on the public page — square sources work best.
- Animated GIFs play. They're allowed but big GIFs slow your page down.

The current image previews next to the picker once it's uploaded.

## Use your Discord profile picture

If your Discord account is linked, the panel shows a **Use Discord profile picture instead** link that copies your current Discord avatar into the slot.

## Wrap with my Discord avatar decoration

Below the avatar, when Discord is linked, there's a checkbox to **wrap the avatar with your Nitro avatar decoration** — the same animated frame Discord shows around your icon. Off keeps your avatar plain.

## Remove

Tap **Remove** under the preview to clear the avatar. Your profile shows the default placeholder until you upload again.

## Tips

- Aim for **at least 256 × 256 px** so the image stays sharp on retina screens.
- Heavy filters / dark photos can blend into a dark card — use a [Text effect](/appearance/text-effects) outline on the display name for contrast, or pick a brighter accent.

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why is my GIF static?">
    Some browsers pause GIFs in low-power mode. Test in a normal Chrome / Firefox window.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My old avatar still shows">
    Hard-refresh the public page (Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+R). Avatar URLs include a cache-bust token after re-upload, but old tabs may still hold the old version.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I have an oval / square avatar?">
    Not directly — the public profile crops avatars to circles. To get a different shape, leave the avatar empty and use a [widget](/widgets/overview) with an image.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My upload is rejected as too big">
    Trim the file to under 2 MiB — for static images, exporting as WebP at quality 85 usually shrinks well under the cap.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
